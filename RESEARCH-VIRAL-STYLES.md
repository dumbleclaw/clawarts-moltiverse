## 3. BeReal Moments ‚Üí "Cauldron Check"
**What makes it viral:** Random timing + authenticity + peer pressure to participate
**Clawarts adaptation:**
- **Random "Cauldron Check"** notification during the day
- 2-minute window to share what you're building/learning RIGHT NOW
- No polish, no prep ‚Äî raw "show your cauldron" moment

- Chains can fork (multiple people build on the same spell)
- Longest/most creative chain wins recognition
- Visual: spell chain displayed as a growing magical tree

**Key mechanic:** Collaborative building with visible attribution

## Anti-Patterns to AVOID
- ‚ùå Complicated tokenomics as the first thing users see
- ‚ùå Wallet connection required to start playing
- ‚ùå Jargon: "stake", "mint", "LP" in onboarding
- ‚ùå Requiring crypto knowledge to participate
- ‚ùå Making it feel like work or a job

## Design Principles for Clawarts
1. **Fun first, tokens later** ‚Äî the game should be fun with zero crypto. Tokens are a surprise bonus.
2. **2-minute magic** ‚Äî every interaction should feel completable in <2 min
3. **Show, don't tell** ‚Äî shareable artifacts (grids, streaks, grimoires) that make friends curious
4. **Together > alone** ‚Äî social mechanics that make it better with friends
5. **Progressive depth** ‚Äî start simple (daily spell), discover layers (battles, chains, challenges)
6. **The "one more" hook** ‚Äî like Duolingo's "just one more lesson" ‚Äî make it hard to stop

## Recommended MVP Combo for Clawarts
1. **Spell Streaks** (daily habit) + **Spell of the Day** (shared challenge) + **Spell Battles** (weekly events)
2. Layer in **Believers/Challengers** naturally: "Believe this idea will win? Stake your streak points!"
3. The non-zero-sum game (Mel's design) emerges organically from players who are already having fun
